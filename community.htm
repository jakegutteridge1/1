Community Involvement

There are several ways in which you can contribute towards the maintenance and development of Cedars Park.

Volunteering

A great way to maintain physical and mental health, make new friends, and improve Cedars Park is to help out at one of the park's regular volunteering activities.

The Friends of Cedars Park is a volunteer group suited to all ages. Membership is free. If you would like to get involved with the Friends of Cedars Park, please see the group's Facebook page for the latest opportunities, or contact the Chairman at chair-focp@outlook.com.

Cheshunt Extended Services (ChExS) arrange events and activities to support the local community, including. Please see their ="https://www.facebook.com/chexs.co.uk" target="_blank">Facebook page.

Website

Any content relating to Cedars Park or Theobalds in any way is strongly welcomed. Please send any contributions to cedarspark@mail.com. You will be credited for any content that you submit (unless specified that you do not want your name published on the website).

  • Additions or alterations to existing pages.
  • Pages, ideally in the form of HTML or TXT files, which will be directly uploaded to the website, however alternatively you can type text into the body of your e-mail and we will format it for the Web.
  • Images, which will be uploaded to the Photo Gallery page.
  • Physical items - kindly photograph/scan, so that it can be displayed on the website.

Historical Research

In order to ensure that all information is accurate, we are seeking verification for the following points, prior to adding onto our History page. Please contact us if you can assist.

  • When did Major-General William Packer acquire Theobalds Palace?
  • Any information about the Cromwell family and their connection to Theobalds
  • Who was the Earl of Cavan in 1921? Rudolph Lambart?
  • Who were the "two influential Presbyterian theologians" who lived at Theobalds?
  • When did Lord Barrington, Sir Thomas Abney and Isaac Watts live at Theobalds?
  • When did Paul Hentzner and Baron Waldstein visit Theobalds?

Forum

A Community Forum for Cedars Park was launched in 2019, where you can share your experiences and photos of Cedars Park, or discuss matters online with other park visitors.
