Welcome to Cedars Park

Cedars Park is a public park located in Cheshunt, Hertfordshire. Once the Royal Palace of Theobalds, which was regularly visited by Queen Elizabeth I and later became King James I's favourite residence, the grounds are of significant historical and national interest. After acquisition by the Meux brewing family, Admiral Sir Hedworth Meux signed a covenant in 1919 that bestowed the grounds as a public park for the people of Cheshunt to enjoy, which was officially opened on 2 July 1921. Over 100 years later, despite busy dual carriageways on two sides, the area survives as a tranquil space for relaxation and leisure.

The park is managed by Broxbourne Borough Council, with the Friends of Cedars Park volunteers providing assistance in maintenance and organising public events. The park has received a Green Flag Award every year since 2009.
