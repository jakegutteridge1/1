Features

Many ruins of Theobalds Palace and subsequent constructions can be found around Cedars Park, and while these were once homes or outbuildings, they now serve as charming focal points for the park, as well as a reminder of the Elizabethan, Jacobean and Georgian architectural styles. Some of the features listed were added in the 2010s after Broxbourne Council received a grant of £1.89 million from the Heritage Lottery Fund.

A Google Earth aerial map of the park's features has been created as a community project, and may be viewed here.

  • Mature and Varied Arboretum
  • Great War Centennial Corner
    War memorial with Representative Tank and Tommy statue.
  • Flint Arch and Huts
    Folly and two huts built in 1765.
  • Maze
    A recent planting based on a maze at Theobalds.
  • Venusberg Mound
    Replica of a garden viewing mound at Theobalds.
  • Bocce Court
    Added in partnership with the Italian town of Sutera, which the Borough of Broxbourne is twinned with.
  • Water
    Theobalds was renowned for its decorative fountains and ponds.
  • Great Garden
    A formal garden from a large complex at Theobalds, surrounded by walls containing bee boles.
  • 17th Century Disability Gardens
    Sensory flowerbeds with scented plants for blind and disabled people.
  • Clay Plaques
    Plaques in memory of significant figures in the park's history.
