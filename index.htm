Cedars Park

Theobalds Lane, Cheshunt, Hertfordshire, EN8 8LS, England

Welcome to Cedars Park. The grounds of this beautiful public park once held Theobalds Palace, a significant royal estate that most notably accommodated Elizabeth I and James I. This was during a time when England was an absolute monarchy, meaning that the country was run from these very grounds. Therefore, the park is of great historical and national interest.

Since the park's opening on 2 July 1921, it has been expanded and now covers around 19 acres. Today, despite busy dual carriageways on two sides, the park retains a tranquil atmosphere, and many ruins of Theobalds Palace and subsequent structures survive, now serving as charming focal points and a reminder of the Elizabethan, Jacobean and Georgian architectural styles - some of these are Scheduled Ancient Monuments.
