Welcome to the Cedars Park Research Website

This site is dedicated to researching the history and features of Cedars Park in Cheshunt, and the Royal Palace of Theobalds, which stood on the grounds until it was largely destroyed in the English Civil War.

  • This is not intended to replace the main park website, cedarspark.org, which should be referred to for visiting information such as opening times and facilities.
  • For community activities and events, please visit the Friends of Cedars Park page on Facebook.
  • To speak with park management, please contact Broxbourne Council.
  • History - A complete timeline from 1383 to the present.

  • Significant Figures - Notable people in Theobalds' history, including photographs of clay plaques in the park.

  • Wildlife - Notable animals and plants in the park.

  • Arboretum - Includes notable tree species, park zones, and details of identification project.

  • Personal Memorials Database - Trees and benches plaqued with the names of those remembered, located by nearby tree numbers.

  • Great War Centennial Corner - War memorial with Representative Tank and Tommy statue.

  • Murder - Newspaper articles about a woman who drowned her son in the Cedars Park pond.

  • Hauntings - Stories of ghosts in the park.

  • Theobalds Square - Residential block built by George Prescott in the late 18th century.

  • Flint Arch and Huts - Folly built in 1765.

  • Maze - A recent planting based on a maze at Theobalds.

  • Venusberg Mound - Replica of a garden viewing mound at Theobalds.

  • Water Features - Theobalds was renowned for its decorative fountains and ponds.

  • Great Garden - A formal garden from a large complex at Theobalds, surrounded by walls containing bee boles.

  • Relationship with Waltham, USA - Cedars Park was used as a venue for meetings between Waltham Cross authorities and people from the American town of Waltham in Massachusetts.

  • 17th Century Disability Gardens - Sensory flowerbeds with scented plants for blind and disabled people.

  • Covenant - The legal agreement for the park, by Admiral Sir Hedworth Meux in 1919.
